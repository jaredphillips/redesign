My advice to those learning how to code

Posted on: May 1st, 2014

Read

Dear Jared,

It is the summer of 2010 right now and you really don't like your job. You've met some friends that have been encouraging you to learn how to code and you've finally decided to take the plunge. Great! I applaud you. It's about time you've done something cool with your life. But there are somethings you should know before you start. These are the things that no one will tell you unless you ask. You don't know it yet, but 'Web Development' is a huge and scary world, I know you won't ask so I'm just going to tell you.

1. Get a mentor

Find someone to help you navigate the murky waters. You'll need it. Meet them as often as you can. Don't be embarassed about not understanding something, ask. Ask repeatedly.

2. Learn HTML & CSS First

Yes, I know you want to learn Ruby or Javascript but your not ready. Make some stuff with this first, get good at it, understand its limitations, be frustrated by them and then and only then, learn another language.

3. Don't get stuck

Your going to having the feeling that you can't move on unless you've understood this key concept. That actually may be true but don't keep bashing your head against the same brick wall. The internet is bursting at the seams in ways of explaining the same thing. Someone else might explain it better or in a way that is more inline with the way you think. If reading about something doesn't work, watch a video. If that doesn't work, try something else! Just don't keep reading that same sentence over and over struggling.

4. Cut Yourself Some Slack

This shit is hard. Most people wouldn't understand half of what you're going to be learning, give yourself a break.

5. It Gets Better

Yes, this shit is hard but it gets better. The learning curve is steep but it does become easier and eventually if you keep at it, the puzzle pieces will start to fit together in surprising ways. But be warned: sometimes it seems like weeks, months and years but you are making progress as long as you keep at it.

6. Have Fun

Remember when you started doing this cause you thought it was going to be fun? Make it fun, don't be so serious about everything and nearly ruin it. Relax and don't be afraid looking like a newb, you are. Share it, embrace it and have fun.
